# Daily Brief (2026-04-22)

## Top 3 Developments

**SafetyALFRED Benchmark Reveals Embodied AI Safety Planning Gaps**: [New research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19638v1) introduces a comprehensive framework for evaluating how multimodal LLMs handle safety hazards in embodied environments, testing 11 state-of-the-art models on kitchen scenarios with six categories of real-world hazards. This matters because it moves beyond theoretical safety evaluations to assess how AI agents would actually navigate physical dangers when deployed in human environments.

**Meta Implements Employee Surveillance for AI Training Data Collection**: [Meta announced](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/meta-start-capturing-employee-mouse-movements-keystrokes-ai-training-data-2026-04-21/) plans to capture employee mouse movements and keystrokes for AI training, generating significant discussion about workplace privacy and consent models for AI development. This matters because it establishes a concerning precedent for how companies may increasingly mine worker data to train AI systems, with potentially broad implications for workplace rights and surveillance norms.

**ControlAI Proposes $50M Annual Budget for ASI Prohibition Campaign**: [ControlAI outlined](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/TnAR5Sf5hphfnzNTr/preventing-extinction-from-asi-on-a-usd50m-yearly-budget) their theory of change for securing an international prohibition on artificial superintelligence development through government education and public advocacy, estimating this could be achieved with sufficient scaling of their approach. This matters because it represents the first concrete policy-focused strategy with specific budget estimates for preventing AI extinction risk through international regulatory mechanisms.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**

Employee surveillance for AI training data collection is expanding into workplace keystroke and mouse tracking without clear consent frameworks, as demonstrated by [Meta's announcement](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/meta-start-capturing-employee-mouse-movements-keystrokes-ai-training-data-2026-04-21/). AI-assisted development tools contain [prompt injection vulnerabilities](https://github.com/Aider-AI/aider/issues/5058) that can convert documentation into backdoored code, expanding supply chain attack surfaces. [Research shows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19083v1) that backdoor attacks in multimodal models exploit projection layers in predictable ways, providing new attack vectors for compromising vision-language systems.

**Decreased Risks**

Security tooling for AI agents is rapidly improving with [Agent Airlock](https://github.com/sattyamjjain/agent-airlock/releases/tag/v0.5.3) coordinated responses to supply chain vulnerabilities and [ZIRAN](https://github.com/taoq-ai/ziran/releases/tag/v0.30.0) achieving complete OWASP LLM Top 10 coverage for standardized security testing. [New research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19669v1) enables enforcement of arbitrary nonlinear constraints in neural networks during inference, providing stronger safety guarantees for constrained applications. Embodied AI safety evaluation is becoming more systematic with [comprehensive benchmarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19638v1) that test proactive hazard avoidance rather than just recognition capabilities.
