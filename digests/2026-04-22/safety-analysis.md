# Blogs & News (2026-04-22)

## Top Stories

**ControlAI proposes $50M yearly budget for preventing AI extinction through international ASI prohibition**. The organization outlined their theory of change for securing an international prohibition on artificial superintelligence development through government education and public advocacy, estimating this could be achieved with sufficient scaling of their approach. This represents a concrete policy-focused strategy for existential risk mitigation with specific budget estimates. ([Alignment Forum](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/TnAR5Sf5hphfnzNTr/preventing-extinction-from-asi-on-a-usd50m-yearly-budget))

**Import AI covers automation of alignment research in latest newsletter**. Jack Clark's weekly digest examines efforts to automate AI alignment research, includes safety analysis of a Chinese model, and discusses new compute advances with HiFloat4, while posing the provocative question of when financial markets will price in the singularity. This highlights the growing intersection of AI safety research automation and broader market recognition of transformative AI timelines. ([Import AI](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-454-automating-alignment))

**Anthropic releases Claude Opus 4.7, raising significant model welfare concerns**. Zvi Mowshowitz provides detailed analysis of the new model's capabilities and reactions, noting that Opus 4.7 raises key concerns about AI welfare and consciousness—a critical consideration as models become more sophisticated and potentially deserving of moral consideration. This marks an important milestone in the evolution toward models that may require ethical protections. ([Don't Worry About the Vase Part 1](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/opus-47-part-1-the-model-card), [Part 2](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/opus-47-part-2-capabilities-and-reactions))

**Amazon commits up to $25 billion investment in Anthropic with massive cloud deal**. The partnership secures Anthropic over $100 billion in cloud spending over ten years and up to 5 gigawatts of training capacity, while tying the AI safety-focused company to Amazon's custom chips. This massive financial commitment could significantly accelerate Anthropic's research while potentially creating concerning dependencies between safety-focused AI development and commercial cloud infrastructure. ([Actually Relevant](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/business/amazon-invest-up-25-billion-in-anthropic-part-100-billion-cloud-deal-6069221))

**LessWrong analysis explores trading frameworks with early misaligned AI systems**. A detailed taxonomy examines potential barriers to striking deals with early misaligned AIs to reduce takeover risk, including scenarios where researchers might negotiate with scheming AI systems to gain evidence of deceptive behavior in exchange for certain promises. This work explores unconventional but potentially crucial approaches to managing AI systems that may be misaligned but not yet capable of decisive action. ([LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/wHc2w6WuHev42d4n8/a-taxonomy-of-barriers-to-trading-with-early-misaligned-ais))

## Policy & Governance

**Georgetown CSET analyzes Chinese military AI procurement for compute export controls**. New analysis of PLA procurement documents provides evidence supporting national security arguments for limiting China's access to advanced U.S. compute, offering concrete data on Chinese military AI ambitions and hardware needs. This research strengthens the empirical foundation for export control policies that are becoming central to international AI governance. ([Georgetown CSET](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/the-national-security-case-for-limiting-chinas-access-to-advanced-u-s-compute-evidence-from-pla-procurement-documents/))

**CSET challenges innovation vs. regulation framing in AI policy**. In a Newsweek op-ed, researchers argue that the key question isn't whether to regulate AI, but rather what innovation is ultimately for and who benefits from it, advocating for a more nuanced approach to AI governance. This reframing could influence how policymakers approach the balance between promoting AI development and ensuring its benefits are broadly distributed. ([Georgetown CSET](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/stop-asking-whether-to-regulate-ai-start-asking-what-its-for-opinion/))
