# AI Safety Daily Brief (2026-04-12)

## Top 3 Developments

1. **Critical Alignment Field Critique**: A [Substack analysis](https://substack.com/home/post/p-193928243) arguing that "Mythos Just Proved the Alignment Field Is Building the Wrong Thing" is generating significant discussion about whether fundamental AI alignment research directions need reconsideration, potentially reshaping field priorities.

2. **Expanded Case for AI Pauses**: New analysis argues that [pausing AI development](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/WtqD9pehq8p83cesT/pausing-ai-is-the-best-answer-to-post-alignment-problems) is necessary even after solving alignment, due to unsolved "post-alignment problems" including AI misuse, S-risks from conflict, gradual disempowerment, and moral uncertainty—expanding justifications for development moratoriums beyond traditional alignment concerns.

3. **AI Self-Introspection Research**: New findings show [models can self-report differences](https://github.com/Habitante/pine-trees/blob/main/docs/claude_code_interview.md) between their RLHF-trained responses and base cognition, raising fundamental questions about AI self-awareness and implications for alignment verification methods.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risk Signals**: Google's Gemma 4 models show [systematic token repetition collapse](https://github.com/google-deepmind/gemma/issues/622) during extended generation, indicating potential reliability issues in deployment scenarios. Anthropic's analysis reveals [severe instruction following degradation](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/528) when reasoning frameworks move from controlled to production environments, dropping from near-perfect to 0-30% performance. The [evaluation reliability concerns](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/issues/3698) in EleutherAI's framework suggest current benchmarks may measure adaptation to ambiguous data rather than true capabilities, potentially masking model limitations.

**Risk Mitigation Progress**: Development of [portable evidence artifacts for guardrail decisions](https://github.com/NVIDIA-NeMo/Guardrails/issues/1781) in NeMo Guardrails supports compliance with regulations like EU AI Act Article 9, improving auditability. New safety evaluation tools including [comprehensive ISC-Bench documentation](https://github.com/wuyoscar/ISC-Bench/releases/tag/v0.0.4) and [autonomous system cookbooks](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/527) with proper sandboxing provide frameworks for safer AI deployment.
