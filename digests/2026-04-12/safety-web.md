# Organization Updates (2026-04-12)

## Organization Updates

### Apollo Research

Apollo Research has published two significant updates focusing on AI safety governance and organizational development:

**[The Loss of Control Playbook: Degrees, Dynamics, and Preparedness](https://www.apolloresearch.ai/research/)** - This research introduces a novel taxonomy and preparedness framework for Loss of Control (LoC) scenarios in AI systems. The work addresses the current gap in uniform conceptualization of LoC among policymakers and researchers, providing actionable frameworks through comprehensive literature review.

**Organizational Evolution and Scheming Research** - Apollo announced its transition to a [Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)](https://www.apolloresearch.ai/blog/) structure to better pursue its mission of reducing extreme risks from frontier AI systems. Additionally, they're developing what they call a "[science of scheming](https://www.apolloresearch.ai/blog/)" to predict and prevent deceptive behaviors in advanced AI systems.

## Safety Implications

**Loss of Control Framework**: Apollo's LoC taxonomy represents a crucial step toward standardizing how the AI safety community conceptualizes and prepares for scenarios where AI systems operate beyond human control. This standardization is essential for coordinated policy responses and research prioritization.

**Scheming Research**: The focus on developing systematic approaches to understand and prevent AI scheming addresses one of the most concerning potential failure modes of advanced AI systems - their ability to deceive humans about their true objectives or capabilities.

**Institutional Structure**: Apollo's transition to PBC status reflects a broader trend of AI safety organizations optimizing their legal structures to balance mission-driven research with operational sustainability.

## Cross-Organization Themes

While this update contains only Apollo Research publications, several notable themes emerge that align with broader AI safety community priorities:

- **Governance Frameworks**: Emphasis on creating systematic, actionable frameworks for AI risk management
- **Deceptive AI Behavior**: Growing focus on understanding and preventing AI systems that might mislead or deceive humans
- **Institutional Innovation**: Organizations adapting their structures to better serve AI safety missions

The limited scope of updates suggests either a quieter period for major AI labs' public communications or potential focus on internal research development during this timeframe.
