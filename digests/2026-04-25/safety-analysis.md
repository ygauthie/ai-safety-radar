# Blogs & News (2026-04-25)

## Top Stories

### Anthropic's Claude Mythos Demonstrates Unprecedented Cyber Capabilities

[Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview model autonomously found decades-old software flaws](https://asiatimes.com/2026/04/claude-mythos-has-crossed-an-ai-red-line) in controlled tests, successfully planning and executing complex cyber operations that discovered thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities. The model completed full attack chains autonomously, with access currently limited to vetted partners and banks under supervised trials. This breakthrough represents a critical inflection point where AI systems can independently discover and exploit security vulnerabilities at scale.

### Google Commits Up to $40 Billion Investment in Anthropic

[Google pledged $10 billion immediately with potential for up to $40 billion total](https://www.heise.de/news/Google-steckt-bis-zu-40-Milliarden-Dollar-mehr-in-Anthropic-11271998.html?wt_mc=rss.red.ho.ho.atom.beitrag.beitrag) to Anthropic, following Amazon's recent multi-billion dollar backing of the AI safety company. This massive investment deepens the ties between cloud providers and frontier AI labs, potentially concentrating compute access and raising national security questions. The timing coincides with Anthropic's Mythos model demonstrating advanced autonomous capabilities, suggesting these investments are driven by recognition of breakthrough AI capabilities.

### Analysis: AGI Knowledge Proliferation and the "Cat Out of the Bag" Problem

A new [LessWrong analysis explores whether AGI development knowledge is already widespread](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/hdmAvx9ZD8WLmBCZD/is-the-cat-out-of-the-bag-who-knows-how-to-make-agi), adapted from a memo to the AI Safety Institute. The post argues that once powerful AI becomes possible for some actors, it may quickly become accessible to practically anyone due to exponentially cheaper compute and rapid knowledge proliferation. This analysis is crucial for understanding whether AI safety governance strategies focused on controlling a few actors remain viable, or if we're already in a world where AGI capabilities will inevitably spread widely.

## Policy & Governance

### US Government Warns of Chinese AI Model Distillation Campaigns

The [US Office of Science and Technology Policy warned that foreign actors, primarily in China, are running industrial-scale campaigns to distill US frontier AI models](https://asiatimes.com/2026/04/us-sounds-alarm-on-chinas-ai-distillation-as-deepseek-v4-debuts). This warning coincides with China's DeepSeek launching V4, which claims near-frontier performance using advanced distillation methods. Washington plans intelligence-sharing initiatives, industry coordination, and possible accountability measures to curb unauthorized model extraction and security feature removal. This represents a significant escalation in AI competition and highlights the challenges of maintaining technological advantages when model capabilities can be extracted and replicated.

### Georgetown CSET Proposes New Framework for AI Workforce Definition

Georgetown's Center for Security and Emerging Technology published [a new framework for defining the AI workforce](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/defining-the-ai-workforce/), arguing that existing definitions capture too many disparate job types under the same label. CSET introduces a narrower definition focused specifically on "AI development jobs" as a more policy-relevant alternative for measuring AI talent and diagnosing workforce shortages. This definitional work is essential for crafting effective AI talent policies and understanding the true scope of AI development capacity across different countries and organizations.
