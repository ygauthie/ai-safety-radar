# Blog Posts & Articles (2026-04-09)

## Top Stories

**Anthropic Releases Claude Mythos with Unprecedented Security Capabilities**
Anthropic has unveiled [Claude Mythos](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/5Dsuw9gGzkbjS4ubx/ai-163-mythos-quest), an AI model that reportedly discovered critical security vulnerabilities in every major operating system and browser. Rather than deploying it directly, Anthropic is launching Project Glasswing to make Mythos available to cybersecurity companies for patching vulnerabilities. This development raises significant questions about AI capabilities in cybersecurity and the potential for both defensive and offensive applications. A [detailed analysis](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/ZfbChZBXgje8T6Geu/excerpts-and-notes-on-mythos-model-card) of the model card highlights various concerning aspects of the system's capabilities.

**AI Safety Community Grapples with "Point of No Return" Question**
A reflection on the current state of AI safety asks whether the community has already reached [a point of no return](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/tjZWbmuCE9tjYQsrf/have-we-already-lost-part-1-the-plan-in-2024) regarding AI existential risk. The post examines whether AGI development has moved beyond effective community control, though the author ultimately concludes the answer is no. This represents broader community concerns about the pace of capability development versus safety progress.

**Public Attitudes Survey Reveals Ground-Level AI Risk Perceptions**
An unprecedented [door-to-door survey](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/BCiDwMbvq5JRNAwwt/101-humans-of-new-york-on-the-risks-of-AI) of 101 New York residents provides rare insight into public understanding of AI risks. The in-person interviews offer a different perspective from typical online polling, revealing how ordinary citizens conceptualize AI's potential impacts outside of academic or policy circles.

**New Book Aims to Build Broader AI Reform Movement**
[Obsolete: The AI Industry's Trillion-Dollar Race to Replace You—and How to Stop It](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2HfuvYzRZgHpLAgpe/help-me-launch-obsolete-a-book-aimed-at-building-a-new) represents an effort to bring AI safety concerns to mainstream audiences, particularly skeptics and progressive activists. The book aims to bridge technical AI safety work with broader social movements focused on AI reform and regulation.

**Security Vulnerabilities Extend to AI Safety Infrastructure**
A [warning from LessWrong administrators](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2wi5mCLSkZo2ky32p/do-not-be-surprised-if-lesswrong-gets-hacked) highlights how AI-discovered security vulnerabilities could affect AI safety community infrastructure. This creates a direct feedback loop where AI capabilities may compromise the very platforms used to discuss AI safety.

## Key Arguments

**Super-Persuasion Capabilities Don't Need to Be Perfect**
A discussion on [AI persuasion capabilities](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JcavsPku6RR9hcujz/slightly-super-persuasion-will-do) argues against the "efficient market for power" view that societies will effectively adapt to AI manipulation. The author contends that even modest improvements in persuasive capabilities could be transformative, pointing to historical examples of powerful influencers as precedent for what AI systems might achieve.

**Dual-Use Dilemma in AI Security Applications**
The Mythos release illustrates a core tension in AI safety: systems capable of finding security vulnerabilities could theoretically be used maliciously. Anthropic's approach of partnering with cybersecurity firms represents one model for handling dual-use AI capabilities, though questions remain about scaling this approach.

## Community Discussion

**Elicitation and Evaluation Challenges**
An [unsupervised elicitation challenge](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/ASoFTyk3bzBE62dyn/my-unsupervised-elicitation-challenge) involving Claude Opus 4.6's performance on Ancient Greek demonstrates ongoing difficulties in properly evaluating AI capabilities without domain expertise. This highlights broader challenges in AI assessment and the importance of diverse evaluation methods.

**Community Identity and Growth**
A [newcomer's perspective](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JAR8b9G4NpNM6seTn/one-week-in-the-rat-farm) on rationalist culture at Lighthaven provides insight into how the AI safety community appears to outsiders and how it's evolving. This represents ongoing questions about community growth, accessibility, and maintaining core values while expanding influence.

The week's developments center heavily around the implications of rapidly advancing AI capabilities, particularly in cybersecurity, and the community's ongoing efforts to maintain relevance and influence as the field evolves. The Mythos release serves as a concrete example of the dual-use challenges that theoretical AI safety work has long anticipated.
