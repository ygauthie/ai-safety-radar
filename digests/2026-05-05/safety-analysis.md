# Blogs & News (2026-05-05)

## Top Stories

**[Import AI 455: AI systems are about to start building themselves](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-455-automating-ai-research)** - Jack Clark's latest newsletter focuses on the automation of AI research itself, describing it as "the first step towards recursive self improvement." This development represents a critical inflection point where AI systems begin contributing directly to their own advancement, potentially accelerating capability gains in unpredictable ways.

**[The distillation panic](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/the-distillation-panic)** - Nathan Lambert examines what he calls the "distillation panic," arguing that "'Distillation attacks' is a horrible term for what is happening right now." This suggests ongoing confusion and potentially misframed discussions around model distillation techniques, which could impact how the AI safety community approaches knowledge transfer between AI systems.

**[Irretrievability; or, Murphy's Curse of Oneshotness upon ASI](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/fbrz9xhKpEeTKw5zL/irretrievability-or-murphy-s-curse-of-oneshotness-upon-asi)** - This LessWrong post explores the challenge of "irretrievability" in advanced AI systems, using analogies like the Viking 1 Mars lander to illustrate how one-shot deployment scenarios create unique risks. The analysis highlights a fundamental challenge in ASI deployment where mistakes may be impossible to correct after the fact.

**[Building a new enterprise AI services company with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs](https://www.anthropic.com/news/enterprise-ai-services-company)** - Anthropic announces a major enterprise AI services venture backed by prominent financial firms, with Applied AI engineers working directly with mid-sized companies to integrate Claude into critical operations. This represents a significant expansion of frontier AI capabilities into enterprise infrastructure, raising questions about deployment oversight and safety protocols at scale.

## Policy & Governance

**[The European Union becomes the Global Coalition on Telecommunications first strategic partner](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/european-union-becomes-global-coalition-telecommunications-first-strategic-partner)** - The EU has joined as the first strategic partner of the Global Coalition on Telecommunications, though details about AI-specific implications remain limited from the brief announcement. This partnership could influence how AI governance frameworks intersect with telecommunications infrastructure regulation globally.

**[Commission services sign cooperation arrangement with Japan's Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-services-sign-cooperation-arrangement-japans-ministry-internal-affairs-and)** - The European Commission has signed a cooperation arrangement with Japan's communications ministry to support digital platform regulation enforcement. This bilateral cooperation could establish precedents for international coordination on AI platform governance and cross-border regulatory enforcement.
