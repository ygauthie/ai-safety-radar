# Journal Articles (2026-04-13)

## Key Articles

**[Interpretability and implicit model semantics in biomedicine and deep learning](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-026-01177-0)** - *Nature Machine Intelligence*

This article examines the critical challenge of model interpretability in biomedical AI applications, where understanding the implicit semantic representations learned by deep learning systems is essential for safe deployment in healthcare settings. The research explores how opaque model semantics can lead to misaligned behavior in high-stakes medical decision-making scenarios, highlighting the need for robust interpretability frameworks that can reveal whether AI systems have learned clinically meaningful patterns versus spurious correlations. The findings have significant implications for AI safety in healthcare, emphasizing that interpretability is not merely a nice-to-have feature but a fundamental safety requirement when AI systems make decisions that directly impact human health and wellbeing.
