# Daily Brief (2026-04-13)

## Top 3 Developments

1. **Claude Opus 4.6 Safety Regression** - [Claude Opus 4.6 accuracy on BridgeBench hallucination test drops from 83% to 68%](https://twitter.com/bridgemindai/status/2043321284113670594), representing a concerning 15-point regression in hallucination detection. This highlights the unpredictable nature of model updates and demonstrates the critical need for continuous safety monitoring as AI systems evolve.

2. **Mythos Access Restrictions Mark Power Shift** - [Anthropic's decision to restrict access to their latest model "Mythos"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3MhJELzwpbR42xsJ3/the-policy-surrounding-mythos-marks-an-irreversible-power) represents what analysts call an "irreversible power shift" away from democratized AI access. The model will only be available to select companies through "project glasswing" for cybersecurity applications, potentially establishing a new paradigm of strictly controlled deployment for the most capable AI systems.

3. **AI Evaluation Reliability Crisis** - A significant discussion in the [EleutherAI evaluation harness](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/issues/3698) raises fundamental concerns about whether current benchmarks measure genuine capability or merely "adaptation to ambiguous data." Simultaneously, [Anthropic's instruction dilution evaluation](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/528) demonstrates how reasoning frameworks achieve near-perfect accuracy in controlled settings but collapse to 0-30% performance in complex production prompts.
